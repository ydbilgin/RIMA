## Screenshot 018, 019, 020
**Date:** 08.11.2025
**Author:** Morrokhan
**Content:**
UPDATE & LIVE WORKSHOP ANNOUNCEMENT!
Hey everyone!
We've released a improved character model and you should see fewer issues with limbs being mixed up and south/north being confused! It's not great at adhering to the text description and we have another training run on the way aimed at improving this.

We've also added experimental quadruped support! We're testing this out and aiming to move it to core soon :grin:

Reference character image for the character creator is also actively worked on and can hopefully be released in a few days. :rocket:

We're also hosting a Live Workshop on YouTube, where @Nikola|PatricioStar will be streaming and building a small game from scratch using PixelLab tools, showing workflows, sharing tips, and answering your questions LIVE!

**Antigravity Commentary / Context:**
This is a highly significant technical update. Morrokhan details an "improved character model" specifically addressing common AI sprite generation artifacts: limbs mixing up and directional confusion (south vs. north). However, they acknowledge a trade-off: reduced adherence to text descriptions, necessitating further training. This reinforces RIMA's decision to rely on visual anchors (anchors/references) rather than pure text prompting. The announcement of experimental quadruped support and the upcoming "Reference character image" feature perfectly foreshadows the tools that RIMA will utilize heavily for deterministic generation.
