# WALLLESS MOCKUP REPORT - laurethayday - 2026-05-25 17:27

## V1 (Hybrid Hades):
Visual strengths: This is the strongest premium read: clear chibi Warblade center, broken columns, cyan runes, warm brazier, and visible cliff edge all support the wall-less arena idea. Gameplay implications: boundaries are readable through cliff edges and sparse collision props without making the space feel boxed in. Asset cost gut-check: medium, because columns, cliff edge tiles, brazier lighting, and rune variants are needed, but density stays controlled.

## V2 (Object Barriers):
Visual strengths: This version feels busy, lived-in, and gameplay-rich, with many readable props and enemies that make the arena feel active. Gameplay implications: object-defined boundaries can create pathing, cover, choke points, and breakables, but they also risk visual clutter and navigation ambiguity. Asset cost gut-check: high, because it needs many prop families, collision variants, destruction states, and placement rules to avoid repetition.

## V3 (Walls + dressing):
Visual strengths: This is the most immediately legible classic dungeon image, with strong room shape, torches, altar, chest, and clean combat floor. Gameplay implications: the complete perimeter wall gives the clearest mechanical boundary and safest player readability, but it does not test the wall-less fantasy. Asset cost gut-check: medium-low for production risk, because walls and a small dressing set can scale reliably across rooms.

## V4 (Negative Space):
Visual strengths: This is the most atmospheric and minimal, with a strong floating-island silhouette, black void, cyan particles, brazier accent, and dash motion. Gameplay implications: the boundary is extremely readable as empty space, which supports dash tactics and fast combat, but sparse rooms may need encounter design to avoid feeling empty. Asset cost gut-check: low-to-medium, because it needs excellent edge tiles, void treatment, and a few hero props more than a broad prop library.

## Visual verdict (purely aesthetic):
V1 - It fits RIMA best visually: premium wall-less arena readability, strong cyan/warm lighting contrast, centered Warblade focus, and enough environmental identity without burying the combat space in props.
