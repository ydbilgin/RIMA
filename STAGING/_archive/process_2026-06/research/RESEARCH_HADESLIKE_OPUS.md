# RESEARCH — Hades-likes → RIMA Adaptation (DESIGN-IDENTITY LENS)
**2026-05-31 · Opus design-identity lens · 3-AI parallel (cx=feasibility, agy=breadth/web) — this doc is the "does it make RIMA *itself*" angle.**

> Method: every candidate judged against RIMA's identity pillars — **Sundered Beat** (BREAK→EXECUTE), **Sundered Brink** floating seal-keep, cyan #00FFCC = seal-energy/reality-cracks, somber-tragic tone ("Vivid Vulnerability"), Penitent Sovereign = boss-as-tragedy, OWNS/AVOIDS class locks, 5-rooms+boss + skill-draft + Map-Fragment→Gate. LOCKED constraints (cursor-aim / floating-island / PixelLab sprites / cyan-sparing / painterly slash) treated as fixed walls.
> Verdict scale: **SHARPENS** (compounds the core identity) · **NEUTRAL** (good hygiene, identity-agnostic) · **DILUTES** (fun elsewhere, off-identity here — a trap).

---

## RANKED LIST

### Tier S — SHARPENS the core (build these; they make RIMA recognizable)

**1. [Hades 1 — Boon "Duo" reveal + curated draft framing] → [Sundered Beat made *legible* in the draft itself] → [Impl S] → SHARPENS**
Hades' draft isn't just "3 cards"; it teaches you the *interaction* (Duos light up when prerequisites are owned, cards show what they pair with). RIMA already has the 3-card draft — what's missing is that the cards *advertise the BREAK→EXECUTE loop*. Map verb to: chain-skills show a cyan link-icon when a partner is owned ("pairs with: Sunder Mark"), and the offer pool is *curated* (~6-8 high-interlock skills, not all 12) so a 10-min run reliably assembles the signature combo. This is the single highest-leverage identity move: it makes the player *see* the thing RIMA is about while choosing. (= M68 synergy-UI in the skill report; aligns with `ChainWindowTracker`.)

**2. [Hades 1 — codified verb on every encounter / "the build does the talking"] → [Sundered as a screaming visual state] → [Impl S] → SHARPENS**
Hades reads instantly because effects are loud and self-evident. RIMA's whole identity is BREAK→EXECUTE, yet Sundered/Broken currently have no prominent tell. A broken enemy MUST scream it: shattered-armor pixel shards (Warblade-exclusive metallic per OWNS lock), a cyan hairline crack-glow on the sprite, and an audio "crack." "You can't combo what you can't see" — and you can't *feel like RIMA* if the world's signature verb is invisible. Cyan-sparing palette is honored because the crack-glow is small/transient (the seal-energy fiction literally is "cracks leaking cyan").

**3. [Curse of the Dead Gods — corruption/risk economy as the run's spine] → [The Brink as a tightening seal: rooms leak more cyan as you push] → [Impl M] → SHARPENS]**
CotDG's Corruption meter is the cleanest "press your luck" engine in the genre, and it's *thematically fused* to the dungeon (greed = corruption). RIMA's fiction is a **breaking seal over a void**. Adapt the *frame*, not the meter: as the run deepens, rooms visibly destabilize — more cyan rift-cracks (room-fill canon already supports this), Rift Tear/Rift Bloom hazards bleed earlier (boss-canon mechanics demoted to room hazards), Risk-Offer draft cards ("accept a Burden for power") map to *accepting more rift leakage*. This turns the 5-room arc into a *narrative of escalating containment failure* — pure RIMA. NOT a separate meter to manage; it's the world reacting. Pairs with the HP-as-economy canon (no rest rooms).

**4. [Hades 2 — the hub as an unfolding character / between-run testimony] → [Ferryman/Vrel/Sister Mourne/Cartographer drip] → [Impl M, mostly content] → SHARPENS]**
RIMA's canon hub NPCs and "Vivid Vulnerability" tone are *already* its strongest differentiator from every twitchy roguelite — observational, grief-tinged, never warm/cold. Hades 2 proves the model: NPCs that say something new each run, dialogue gated on run-count (Ferryman "sits down by your 9th run"), meta-currency framed as *recovering your own forgotten faces* (Shattered Echoes). This is the meta-progression hook that is on-identity. The design move: make *every* between-run touch advance one thread by one line — cheap, compounding, and unmistakably RIMA. Most reference games (Gungeon, RoR2, Wizard of Legend) have *no* tonal voice; this is RIMA's moat.

**5. [Dead Cells / Skul — moveset *identity* per loadout, enforced] → [OWNS/AVOIDS as the anti-clone law] → [Impl: it's a design *gate*, not code; S to enforce] → SHARPENS]**
Dead Cells weapons and Skul's heads each *play* distinctly; the failure mode of breadth (10 classes) is that they blur into "different colored damage." RIMA's OWNS/AVOIDS matrix (Warblade owns metallic Sundered; Brawler's armor-break MUST be organic hairline fissures, never shards) is the discipline that prevents this — and it's *already canon*. The adaptation idea is to treat OWNS/AVOIDS as a hard *approval gate* (a skill that borrows another class's verb or VFX is rejected), the way Skul guards each head's silhouette/feel. This protects identity at the exact place 10-class scope threatens it.

### Tier A — SHARPENS with care (strong, but watch the scope/identity edge)

**6. [Hades — bespoke mini-narrative bosses / boss-as-character] → [Penitent Sovereign as tragic-mirror, phases = emotional beats] → [Impl M] → SHARPENS]**
Hades bosses *say something* (Megaera, Theseus). RIMA's Penitent Sovereign is canon-designed as tragedy: chained = self-discipline, 66% "...no longer enough," chains snap, 33% body becomes corruption. The identity move is to make each phase transition an *authored emotional beat* (camera pause, music break, the cyan whisper) rather than a stat threshold — boss-as-tragedy is the BREAK→EXECUTE verb told at the level of a character (he is a thing that breaks, and you witness it). Keep demo at Phase-1 placeholder; the *direction* is locked. DILUTES only if it becomes a generic 3-phase HP-gate without the narrative framing.

**7. [Returnal / Hades — escalating-intensity boss telegraphs + i-frame dash as the answer-verb] → [dash i-frames as the BREAK-setup, not just survival] → [Impl S-M] → SHARPENS/NEUTRAL]**
RIMA has cursor-aim + dash i-frames (locked). Returnal/Hades teach that the dash should be *offensively integrated*, not a panic button. Map: dash-cancel into the commit-beat (canon "animation cancel dash-strike"), and dash-through a Sundered enemy as an execute setup. This keeps the locked dash but makes it part of the rhythm verb rather than a separate survival system. NEUTRAL if treated as pure mobility; SHARPENS when it feeds the 2-beat.

**8. [Hades — Heat/Pact of Punishment difficulty escalation] → [post-demo replay knobs, NOT demo] → [Impl M] → NEUTRAL→SHARPENS later]**
Hades' Heat system is *the* retention engine and fits RIMA's "the rift adapts to you" fiction (canon Grudge/Nemesis: bosses adapt to how you kill them). But it's Phase-5 (4 difficulty modes already in canon). For the demo it's a trap (scope). Flagged here so the *direction* is reserved: difficulty modifiers should be themed as *the seal weakening further*, not generic "+20% enemy HP."

**9. [Dead Cells — biome *branching* via Map Fragment foresight] → [StS-style "see the next 1-2 rooms" build-planning] → [Impl: already canon] → SHARPENS]**
RIMA's Map Fragment already reveals next 1-2 room types (StS-style) so you draft *toward* an upcoming Elite. Dead Cells' branching paths validate this as a depth engine. The identity nuance: keep it *legible and short* (5 rooms, linear-with-foresight in demo) — RIMA is a tight 10-min tragedy, not a sprawling map. The reveal is also *narrative* ("gathering the visual narrative of a shattered world"). Confirm, don't expand.

**10. [Children of Morta / Bastion — environmental + narrated storytelling over cutscenes] → [cyan typewriter whispers + rune-lore fragments] → [Impl S, content] → SHARPENS]**
Bastion's reactive narrator and Morta's family-tragedy framing are the tonal cousins of "Vivid Vulnerability." RIMA's canon already specifies cyan typewriter boss-whispers on room entry and 3 interactive rune-lore fragments. This is *cheap* (no VO) and *exactly* the tone. The trap to avoid: a full Bastion-style live narrator is over-scope and risks breaking the "the game merely observes, never comforts" rule — keep it sparse, momentary, observational.

### Tier B — NEUTRAL (genre hygiene; do them well, but they don't define RIMA)

**11. [Hades / all — hit-stop, screen-shake, hit-flash, damage numbers ("juice")] → [game-feel layer] → [Impl: mostly done] → NEUTRAL]**
Canon-locked already (0.04/0.12s hit-stop tiers, directional shake, white flash, floating numbers). Necessary for the "first 60 seconds" wishlist goal but identity-agnostic — every good action game has this. Do it; don't mistake it for identity.

**12. [Enter the Gungeon / Tiny Rogues — input buffering, animation cancel, coyote time, hitbox leniency] → [fluidity layer] → [Impl S, partly canon] → NEUTRAL]**
Canon fluidity rules exist. Genre table stakes. NEUTRAL — but note the *cursor-aim* lock means RIMA leans melee-rhythm, NOT bullet-hell; don't import Gungeon's dodge-roll-spam pacing.

**13. [Risk of Rain 2 — item stacking / exponential build snowball] → [tier upgrades on owned skills] → [Impl S] → NEUTRAL]**
RIMA's Common/Rare/Epic/Legendary upgrade-on-owned-skill model is a contained version of RoR2's stacking. Fine as a draft category. NEUTRAL — RoR2's *unbounded* stacking would break RIMA's deliberate-rhythm pacing (see Traps).

### Tier C — DILUTES (popular elsewhere, a TRAP for RIMA)

**14. [Risk of Rain 2 — time-pressure escalation / "the longer you take, the harder it gets" clock] → DILUTES]**
RoR2's difficulty-over-time clock forces *frantic rushing*. RIMA's Rage already decays and the canon explicitly wants "deliberate swing frames, not a frantic rush" (round-1 finding: Rage decay was *too* aggressive). A run-wide clock would actively fight the Sundered Beat's 2-beat deliberation and the somber-witness tone. TRAP.

**15. [Enter the Gungeon / Wizard of Legend — bullet-hell density + dodge-roll-centric defense] → DILUTES]**
RIMA is cursor-aim *melee-rhythm* (Warblade approach/pin/break/execute). Bullet-hell screen-saturation and roll-spam defense are a different game; they'd drown the readability of Sundered states and the painterly slash VFX, and contradict the "deliberate" combat pillar. Cursor-aim is for *targeting the break*, not weaving bullet curtains. TRAP.

**16. [Wizard of Legend / RoR2 — "more is more" ability/item count] → DILUTES]**
The genre's instinct is breadth (Wizard of Legend's 100+ arcana). RIMA *already over-produced* breadth (76 skills) and starved its depth. Canon locks 12/class for a reason: combo richness = interaction density, not skill count. Adding skills *lowers* the interlock ratio. The whole skill-report verdict is "depth not breadth." Every "add more skills" impulse is a TRAP. TRAP.

**17. [Hades / most — free healing fountains / rest rooms / "safe room" loop] → DILUTES]**
Canon explicitly *bans* healing fountains and rest rooms; HP is a managed economy (lifesteal, elite drops, shop, accepting risk). A comfortable rest room would deflate the "containment is failing, every choice is under pressure" tension that the Brink fiction depends on. The genre default is a trap here. TRAP.

**18. [Tiny Rogues / Gungeon — gear/inventory/equipment grids] → DILUTES]**
Stat-grid equipment management is off-identity for a draft-only, no-skill-tree game. Canon "NO skill trees, run-based draft only." An inventory screen would fragment the clean BREAK→EXECUTE read into spreadsheet optimization. TRAP.

**19. [Ravenswatch / Children of Morta — co-op / multiple-heroes-per-run] → DILUTES (for now)]**
Co-op is a different design axis entirely; RIMA's tone ("the game observes *you*," solitary grief, recovering *your* faces) and the single-witness narrative are inherently solo. Multiplayer would dilute the intimacy that is the tonal moat. Not a demo or near-term consideration. TRAP for identity.

## COHERENT DIRECTION — the 3-5 ideas that compound

Pick these and they reinforce each other into one recognizable game:

1. **Make the Sundered Beat *legible* (Tier S #1 + #2):** loud Sundered visual tell + draft cards that advertise the chain. The verb you feel every ~3s is also the verb you *see* and *draft toward*.
2. **Make the Brink *react* to the run (Tier S #3):** rooms leak more cyan / hazards intensify as you push deeper — risk economy fused to the seal fiction, no separate meter, no rest rooms (HP stays an economy).
3. **Make the world *observe* you (Tier S #4 + Tier A #10):** sparse cyan typewriter whispers in-run + an unfolding hub of grief-tinged NPCs between runs, meta-currency framed as recovering your own forgotten faces.
4. **Make the bosses *tragedies* (Tier A #6):** phase transitions are authored emotional beats, not stat gates — the Penitent Sovereign *breaks* in front of you, the verb told at character scale.
5. **Guard it all with OWNS/AVOIDS (Tier S #5):** every class's verb and VFX is exclusive; this is the discipline that keeps 10 classes from blurring as scope grows.

**Pitch — what RIMA becomes if you build these:** RIMA is the roguelite where combat is a held breath and a single, witnessed break. You don't spam — you *approach, pin, shatter the guard (a loud crack of cyan and metal), and execute*, and the run is a 10-minute tragedy of a seal failing around you: every room leaks a little more rift, every draft card sharpens one signature blow, every death the world coldly notes and remembers. Between runs a quiet keep of grieving figures gives back, one line at a time, the faces you've forgotten. It is Hades' legibility and Bastion's grief, fused to one verb — *break, then execute* — over a void that is slowly, beautifully coming apart. No other roguelite *feels* like mourning while it teaches you to hit harder.

## TRAPS TO AVOID (short list)
- **RoR2 run-clock / time-pressure** → fights the deliberate 2-beat + somber tone.
- **Bullet-hell + roll-spam** (Gungeon/Wizard of Legend) → drowns Sundered readability; cursor-aim is for *targeting the break*, not weaving curtains.
- **"More skills/arcana"** (Wizard of Legend) → lowers interlock ratio; canon locks 12/class. Depth, not breadth.
- **Rest rooms / free heal fountains** (Hades default) → canon-banned; deflates containment-failing tension.
- **Inventory/equipment grids** (Tiny Rogues) → contradicts draft-only, no-skill-tree identity.
- **Co-op / multi-hero** (Ravenswatch) → dilutes the solitary-witness tonal moat.
- **Heat/Pact difficulty in the DEMO** (Hades) → right idea, wrong phase; reserve for Phase-5 themed as "the seal weakening."
