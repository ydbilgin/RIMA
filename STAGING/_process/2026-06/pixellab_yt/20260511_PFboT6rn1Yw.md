Did you know you can make Game Boy Advance style pixel art for your indie games using this one tool? It's called pixellab.ai. You just describe your character like a top-down retro fisherman, [music] and it instantly generates a full eight-directional sprite sheet. I love using this as a workflow enhancer. It gets you past the blank canvas phase super fast, and then you just jump in to manually clean up the pixels and perfect the design. You can even animate it directly on the site. Pro tip: for a super smooth walk cycle, don't start with a standing frame. Pick a frame where the legs are already mid-stride. Pair those up with some generated [music] tile sets and map objects, and you've got a beautiful retro world. If you want to see my exact step-by-step workflow for all of this, check out the full tutorial on our YouTube channel.
