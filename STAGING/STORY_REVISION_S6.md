## 1. PREMISE — what the Sundered Brink is

The world did not break by accident. To stop the **Rift March** — an outside void that consumes reality from world to world — every connection between worlds was severed in one irreversible act: **The Fracturing**. The price was accepted; the price came due larger than anyone reckoned. **RIMA** is the central seal-ritual built to hold that march back, and the play-floor is a surviving fragment of its containment keep — a severed structural shard called **The Sundered Brink**, left suspended over the void when the connections were cut.

It does not float by magic or by sky-island whimsy. **It floats because it is anchored to the active seal, not to ground** — the same seal that holds the Rift March back also holds the broken keep aloft. Cliffs are torn edges, the void below is the cut, and the cyan `#00FFCC` bleeding through cracks, rune-circles, and cliff rims is the seal's own energy showing through the wounds. You walk the Brink to keep that seal counted — and dying does not lose the run; it re-counts the seal and sends you back to the threshold.

## 2. THE PLAYER'S ROLE + THE SUNDERED ECHOES (cross-class made canon)

You are **both the one who fractured the world and its last surviving fragment** — perpetrator and remnant of The Fracturing. You carry the weight of the choice but have forgotten its reasons; you wander the keep as a shifting, incomplete self. Hero, criminal, victim — deliberately ambiguous. When The Fracturing tore the connections, it also blasted apart **who you were**: the combat identities you once held scattered into the void as **Shattered Echoes** — forgotten faces of yourself.

This is where the now-LIVE **cross-class mechanic becomes story, not bolt-on.** Calling a cross-class skill is **calling a Sundered Echo of another hero to strike once.** A black silhouette of the guest class forms (cold cyan rim as it coalesces from the seal's energy), performs a single remembered move, and dissolves back into the rift. In fiction: *you reach into the scattered faces of yourself, borrow one forgotten hand to land one blow, and let it go.* The transient, single-strike, non-persistent design IS the fiction — an Echo is a recovered fragment, not a companion; it can only manage one act before the Brink takes it back. As the larger game opens (Phase-2), spending Shattered Echoes to permanently unlock a class is the same act at scale: **reintegrating a lost face you sacrificed to seal the world.** The demo only previews this — one borrowed Echo, not the full reintegration.

## 3. THE SUNDERED BEAT AS FICTION — what "break then execute" means in-world

The signature combat verb is the **Sundered Beat: BREAK the guard, then EXECUTE.** This is not just a combo shape — it is the logic of the whole world rendered at sword-scale. Everything on the Brink is held together by a kind of guard: the seal holds the keep, the Sovereign's chains hold the corruption, an enemy's armor holds its form. **To act on the Brink is to crack a seal and then strike through the wound it leaves.**

Mechanically (canon Warblade arc — *Approach · Pin · Armor-break · Execute*): you build pressure, land the armor-break that drops an enemy into the loud **Sundered/Broken state** (shattered-armor shards, a cyan hairline crack-glow, an audible "crack"), then **Execute** through that crack for bonus damage — and the aggression pays you back (a chip of recently-lost HP and Rage refunded on the execute, on-canon via Battle Surge / Death Blow). The cyan crack on a Sundered enemy is **the same cyan as the seal's wounds**: when you break a foe, you are briefly opening a small rift in it, and the strike that follows is you reaching through. The verb you *feel* in every kill is the verb the whole story is about — *something held; you broke it; you struck the wound.*

## 4. THE PENITENT SOVEREIGN — the tragedy

The Brink's guardian is the **Penitent Sovereign**, and he is the demo's moral weight, never a conquering villain. He was the **former guardian of this fragment of the keep.** When The Fracturing failed to hold cleanly and rift energy began leaking through this section, he did the only thing a guardian could: as self-punishment for what the severing cost, **he absorbed the leaking Rift energy into his own body to keep this part of the seal from failing.** The massive chains he wears are not restraints imposed on him — they are the **metaphor for his self-discipline, the will holding the corruption IN.** "Bowed, but unbroken."

His fight is a tragedy told in BREAK→EXECUTE — the same verb, at character scale, turned mournful:
- **Phase 1 — Under the Chain (100%→66%):** he fights *bound*, restrained, every surge of power pulled back by his own discipline. He is winning the harder fight, the one against himself.
- **Phase 2 — The Broken Chain (66%/demo-50%):** his concentration fails. The chains snap. A volatile **Rift Tear** opens as he leaks the energy he can no longer contain. Beat line: *"Discipline breaks before the chain does."* This is the BREAK — and the tragedy is that **you** caused it: you are forcing the guard to crack.
- **Phase 3 — Sovereign Awakened (33%):** the last chains melt; half his body becomes pure rift. He succumbs — not to you, but to the corruption he held back for so long. The EXECUTE is hollow: defeating him means breaking the very thing keeping this fragment stable.

The phase-breaks are **authored emotional beats** (camera pause, music break, a cold cyan whisper — *"My chains loosen with your steps..."*), the Sundered Beat verb told as character drama. The player wins by un-making a penitent man's discipline. There is no cheer in it.

## 5. RUN ARC AS ESCALATING CONTAINMENT FAILURE ("the Brink reacts")

The 5-room run is not a difficulty ramp dressed as rooms — it reads as **the seal failing further with every step you take.** The Brink reacts to your descent: the deeper you push, the more the keep visibly destabilizes. This is **progress-driven, not clock-driven** (no run-timer — that would fight the deliberate, somber tone).

| Room | Containment state | What the player reads |
|---|---|---|
| R1 — Broken Entry Gate | Seal intact but cracked; calm, dim cyan rune-circle | "This place was held. Something keeps it." Teaches *cyan = boundary/energy* wordlessly. |
| R2 — Guard Hall | First strain; one warm vigil-ember still kept | *"Someone kept this fire. Long after the order fell."* A vigil persists where the order died. |
| R3 — Ambush Cloister | Densest rift-veins; torn structure, hanging chains | Chains foreshadow the Sovereign; the seal is leaking worst here. *"The chains here lead somewhere."* |
| R4 — Map-Fragment Vestibule | Quiet eye-of-the-storm; safe beat, no mobs | Draft = **remembering** a past self. *"You knew this once."* The calm before the containment gives out. |
| R5 — Penitent Containment | Total failure point; the seal's wound is the Sovereign himself | Boss. *"The Sovereign's breath is colder here."* Killing him = the seal here finally fails. |

Per-room cyan leakage escalates (calm → veined → torn) so the arc *looks* like a tightening, then breaking, seal even before any deeper "Brink-reacts" mechanical layer (Phase-2) exists. The Map-Fragment beat fits the fiction: each fragment you place "completes the seal's pattern," tearing open the cyan rift-threshold to the next room — **you are spending the seal's own pattern to go deeper, accelerating its failure.** Reaching the Sovereign and breaking his chains is the run-arc's BREAK→EXECUTE writ large: the whole Brink is the guard, and you are cracking it open room by room.

## 6. VICTORY & DEATH FRAMING (in-world lines, tone-locked)

## 7. DELTA — what changed vs the old story, + open questions

**What this revision changes / locks (vs. pre-session story):**
1. **Cross-class is now narrative, not a loose mechanic.** Old story had Shattered Echoes only as *meta-currency for permanent class unlocks*. Now the **in-run cross-class Echo summon** (live this session) is explicitly the *same fiction at single-strike scale* — calling a forgotten face for one borrowed blow. The mechanic and the lore now reinforce each other instead of coexisting.
2. **"Sundered Beat" is promoted to the story's central metaphor.** The combat verb (BREAK→EXECUTE) is now framed as the world's own logic (seal/guard/armor = things held; you crack then strike the wound), and the enemy Sundered crack-glow is tied to the seal's cyan wounds. Previously the verb lived only in the mechanics report.
3. **The run is reframed as escalating containment failure** ("the Brink reacts to your descent" — progress-driven, no run-clock), tying the 5-room arc, the per-room cyan-leak escalation, and the Map-Fragment "completing the seal's pattern" into one causal story. Previously rooms were just a difficulty sequence.
4. **The Sovereign tragedy is sharpened and made the player's own doing** — the phase-breaks are authored emotional beats, and victory is explicitly hollow (you un-make a penitent's discipline; the seal here fails). Confirms tragic-guardian framing, kills any conquering-villain read.
5. **Floating-island contradiction stays resolved** — never "magically floats / sky-island"; always "severed seal-keep pinned by the active seal," cyan = seal energy bleeding through wounds. Consistent with all art/lighting/map locks.
6. **Demo scope discipline reaffirmed** — Nexus Core, Rift March endgame, full meta-hub, and full class-unlock memory stay OUT. The demo lands only: ambiguous identity + first loop + one borrowed Echo.
