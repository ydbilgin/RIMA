# Project Story / Lore

Use when: story, lore, RIMA meaning, Rift March, final boss reveal, ending hook,
narrative phase split.

## RIMA / Rift March Candidate

Status:
- Candidate narrative direction, not locked.

Branding rule:
- Main logo should read cleanly as `RIMA`.
- Do not force `RIft MArch` hidden-letter logic into the logo if it harms readability.
- Keep `Rift March` as lore/story meaning instead of literal logo text.

Story use:
- `Rift March` can be the name of the world event: rifts open and outside forces begin
  marching into reality.
- `RIMA` can be the central seal, city, machine, ritual, or old command system built to delay
  or contain that march.
- Player may first treat RIMA as only the game/title/place name; later acts reveal its connection
  to the Rift March.

Final boss hook:
- Final boss may not be the true source of the catastrophe.
- Candidate reveal: killing the final boss removes the last lock/warden holding the Rift March
  back.
- Ending can open a final choice, post-game state, or sequel/season hook:
  - replace the lock
  - break the cycle and face the march
  - redirect or seal the march at a cost

## Phase-Level Narrative Split

Phase 1:
- Treat `RIMA` as the game/place/symbol.
- Only visual hints: central rift, cracked stone, ruined march path, repeated seal marks.

Phase 2:
- Add light lore fragments in Act 1 full loop.
- NPC/room fragments can mention an old march, a sealed wound, or a command called RIMA.

Phase 3:
- With Act 2 and dual-class break, reveal that RIMA is not just a place.
- Start implying it is a seal/mechanism connected to repeated runs and echo imprints.

Phase 4:
- With Act 1-2 full and Fracture Echoes, make bosses feel like locks, echoes, or wardens.
- Reveal that defeating echoes can weaken containment as much as it gives power.

Phase 5:
- With Act 1-2-3 + Final, expose the full `Rift March` meaning.
- The Architect/final boss can reveal that the march was delayed, redirected, or harvested by
  RIMA.
- Final boss death can trigger the last reveal and optional ending/post-game branch.
